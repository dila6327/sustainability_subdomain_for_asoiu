Sustainability Training Programmes

All members of society share a responsibility in advancing sustainability and safeguarding the environment, thereby contributing to a healthier and more equitable world for current and future generations.

At ASOIU, there is a strong institutional commitment to improving environmental performance. This objective can only be achieved through the collective efforts of the entire academic community. Students and staff are encouraged to develop their understanding of sustainability and climate change through a range of educational programmes designed to strengthen awareness and responsible behaviour.

Research has shown that participation in educational activities contributes not only to increased knowledge, but also to improved psychological well-being, higher life satisfaction, and a stronger sense of personal effectiveness.

Staff Environmental Sustainability Training

An online training module has been developed to equip university staff with the knowledge and skills required to contribute positively to environmental sustainability.

The module explains the environmental and energy impacts associated with everyday university activities, clarifies individual responsibilities, and highlights the importance of collective action.

Completion of this training is compulsory for all staff members.

  • Undertaken during the induction process for new staff
  • Monthly central induction sessions include a sustainability presentation delivered by the Sustainable Development Office
  • Staff must complete 18 hours of sustainability-related training annually

Module topics include:

  • The University’s Sustainability Vision
  • Sustainable Travel and low-carbon transport initiatives
  • Energy and water efficiency strategies
  • Resource use, recycling and waste management

Social Sustainability Training

To support inclusive and equitable practices, ASOIU provides an additional training module focusing on the social dimensions of sustainability.

This training explores connections between sustainability and:

  • Social justice and equity
  • Diversity and inclusion
  • Human rights in the workplace
  • Health and community wellbeing
  • Ethical leadership

The programme aligns with global sustainability frameworks such as the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs 3, 4, 5 and 10).

  • Completion time: 30–40 minutes
  • Certification issued upon completion
  • Training recorded automatically in the LMS

Student Sustainability Training

Sustainability education is integrated into the student induction programme to ensure that all new students understand the University’s sustainability goals and policies.

The University promotes sustainability awareness through:

  • Welcome Week activities organised by the Student Alliance for Sustainability
  • Sustainability information included in the Student Welcome Booklet
  • Short training sessions on climate change and environmental responsibility
  • Awareness campaigns organised throughout the academic year
  • The module can be started at any time
  • Progress can be saved and resumed later
  • Average completion time: 20 minutes
  • Training status automatically recorded in the LMS

Impact of Sustainability Training

These training programmes have significantly increased sustainability awareness among both students and staff in recent years.
